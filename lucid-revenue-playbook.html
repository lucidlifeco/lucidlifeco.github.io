Your One Buyer

Most clean brand founders describe their ideal customer as "health-conscious consumers aged 25–45." That's a demographic. It's not a buyer. This module gives you the precision that changes every conversation you have from here forward.

▶
Module 01 Walkthrough — Your One Buyer 8 min
Step 1

Name the person, not the segment.

Your ICP isn't a demographic. It's a person — with a job, a specific frustration, a specific cost of doing nothing, and a specific reason they'd trust you over the alternative. The more precisely you can describe this person, the more specifically you can speak to them. And specificity is what closes.

Before → After

Before: "Health-conscious women 30–45 who care about clean ingredients."

After: "A 38-year-old functional wellness practitioner who recommends products to clients and has been burned twice by brands that greenwashed their sourcing. She needs third-party verified proof before she'll put her name on anything."

◈  Worksheet 1.1 — Your Buyer Profile
If you can describe this in your buyer's exact words, you've found it.
Step 2

Identify the identity trigger.

The most durable buyers don't just have a problem — they have an identity at stake. Your clean brand buyer isn't just solving a sourcing problem. They're protecting who they are. Who does your buyer think they are when they buy from you? Name the identity, and you've found the deepest lever in the sale.

◎
The Identity Test Finish this sentence about your buyer: "When I buy from this brand, I am the kind of person who ___." The answer is your identity trigger. If you can reflect that back in your messaging, you stop selling and start resonating.

Your Gap Statement

One sentence. It names the distance between where your buyer is and where they need to be. When you get this right, it becomes the engine of every sales conversation, every piece of outreach, and every content decision you make.

▶
Module 02 Walkthrough — The Gap Statement 7 min
The Framework

Problem → Impact → Solution. In that order.

A gap statement is not a tagline. It's not a value proposition. It's the sentence that makes your buyer think: "That is exactly my situation." It works because it names their problem specifically, connects it to a real cost, and implies there's a better state available. Most founders lead with the solution. Buyers don't care about the solution until they feel understood.

Gap Statement Formula

[Buyer type] who [specific current problem] — which is [specific cost or consequence] — and [the gap you bridge].

Real Example — Clean Supplement Brand

"Functional wellness practitioners who recommend supplement brands to clients without being able to verify the sourcing claims — which erodes their professional credibility every time a client does their own research — and haven't found a brand that treats third-party certification as the floor, not a marketing badge."

◎
The Resonance Test Read your gap statement out loud to someone who matches your ICP. If they say "that's exactly it" — you have it. If they nod politely, you're still too generic. Keep sharpening until you get the visceral reaction.
◈  Worksheet 2.1 — Build Your Gap Statement
This sentence leads every outreach message, discovery call opening, and sales page from here forward.
Where the playbook ends
A gap statement that feels right internally often doesn't land in the market.

The most common failure here: founders write a gap statement that describes their product's category, not their buyer's actual experience. It sounds right internally. It doesn't stop the scroll. A custom engagement takes your gap statement into real outreach, measures how buyers respond, and refines it until it consistently produces the visceral reaction — "that's exactly my problem."

Module 03

Your Lead Generation Engine

Referral-dependent revenue is not a pipeline. It's a prayer. This module builds the infrastructure to generate consistent conversations — without a cold list, a large audience, or a sales team.

▶
Module 03 Walkthrough — Lead Generation Engine 10 min
Step 1

Map your five warmest doors.

Not a list of industries. Not a cold database. Five actual people or brands already in your orbit who have the highest probability of moving. You likely already know who they are. Most founders don't act on them because they're waiting until everything is perfect. Nothing is ever perfect. Start with the warmest door you have.

◈  Worksheet 3.1 — Your Five Warmest Doors
Step 2

Build your outreach message.

One message. Built on your gap statement. Written to start a conversation — not close a deal. The goal of outreach is a meeting, not a sale. Most founders make their first message too long, too feature-heavy, and too obviously a pitch. Lead with the gap. Keep it short. Ask one question.

Outreach Template — Warm Contact

"Hi [Name] — [one sentence of genuine connection]. I've been working with [brand type] founders on a problem I suspect you've run into: [gap statement in two sentences]. Would it make sense to spend 20 minutes comparing notes? No agenda beyond that."

◈  Worksheet 3.2 — Your First Outreach Message
Read it back. If it sounds like a pitch, rewrite it. If it sounds like a genuine conversation opener, send it.
Step 3

Build your follow-up cadence.

Most founders follow up once, get no response, and go quiet. That's where deals die. A non-response is not a no. It's noise. The cadence below keeps you visible without becoming a pest. Five touches over 30 days. After that, you move on — but you've earned the right to have tried.

◎
The 5-Touch Cadence Day 1: First outreach. Day 4: Short follow-up with one new value point. Day 10: Share a relevant piece of content or observation — no ask. Day 18: One final direct ask. Day 30: Graceful close — "Leaving the door open" message. Then move on and let inbound do its work.
◈  Worksheet 3.3 — Your 30-Day Outreach Plan
Where the playbook ends
Outreach built in isolation often breaks down in execution.

The message template here is a starting point. What breaks down in real outreach: the connection sentence feels forced, the gap statement is still too generic for this specific person, or the follow-up timing is off for your specific industry's buying cycle. A custom engagement reviews your actual outreach before you send it — and tells you what to fix before it costs you the relationship.

Present and Close

A discovery conversation that doesn't feel like a sales call. A presentation that doesn't feel like a pitch. A close that doesn't feel like a close. This is the module most founders skip — and where most deals are lost.

▶
Module 04 Walkthrough — Present & Close 12 min
The Discovery Framework

Ask before you answer.

Most founders arrive at a discovery call ready to present. The buyer hasn't asked you to present yet. The entire purpose of discovery is to understand the gap so precisely that when you do present, you're reflecting the buyer's own situation back to them — not pitching features at them. Questions close deals. Presentations open objections.

Discovery Call Structure — 30 Minutes

0–5 min: Set the agenda and earn the right to ask questions.
5–20 min: Gap questions — current state, desired state, cost of gap, previous attempts to fix it, what's stopped them.
20–25 min: Present only what directly addresses the gap they described. Nothing else.
25–30 min: Ask for the next step — not the sale.

◈  Worksheet 4.1 — Your Discovery Question Bank
Where the playbook ends
Discovery frameworks work in theory. Real calls are messier.

The questions in this module are the right questions. What a playbook can't do is tell you how to redirect when a buyer goes off-script, how to handle an objection that appears mid-discovery, or why your close question keeps getting a "let me think about it." Those patterns only become visible across multiple real calls — and they require an outside eye to diagnose.
